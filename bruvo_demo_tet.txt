/*~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
This is a demonstration of Bruvo's distance comparing 4 tetraploid individuals 
at a single locus. You can find the equations in Bruvo et al. 2004, but for the
most part, the important thing to remember is that Bruvo's distance first does
a pairwise comparison between individuals, and it creates a distance matrix from
the square comparisons over all alleles. After that, it has to find the minimum
average distance over all alleles meaning that it has to go through n!
combinations to access each allele once (It will be demonstrated below). In
terms of the distance matrix, this means that each time you choose a number for
the average, the the row and column must be unique (like a diagonal through the
matrix). Every time an average distance is taken, it is evaluated if it is the
smallest distance observed. At the end of the permutations, the distance is
added to the pairwise comparison array. 
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~*/
